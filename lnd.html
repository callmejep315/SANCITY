LEARNING AND DEVELOPMENT

Project STEER

(STrengthening Employees' Efficacy through excellent Reforms)

Steadfast in navigating across its improved roadmap in the delivery of quality basic education, the SDO Santiago City intensifies the continuous development of all its teaching and non-teaching frontrunners through its project STEER: Strengthening Employees' Efficacy through excellent Reforms.

Trailblazing to develop its workforce's capabilities, skills, and competencies, STEER, in consonance with RA9155 otherwise known as the Governance Basic Education Act of 2001, this division unswervingly creates platforms and opportunities to ensure that everyone is competent in their current role and can respond to the challenge of the fast-paced changing world.

Empowered to bolster the many facets that constitute the growth of the department, this policy prioritizes the reskilling and upskilling of its multigenerational workforce through strategic planning, unfailing implementation and coherent monitoring and evaluation.

Emanated from the department's vision and mission to cater the unique needs and interests of 40, 386 Santiague√±o learners to allow the achievement of their full potential and the development of their well-being, the Human Resource and Development Committee (HRDC) frameworks capability developments and well-defined structures and roles.

Road mapping to quality basic education, STEER: the L & D Program of SDO - Santiago City is anchored on the PRIME-HRM standards for L and D which promotes the integration of learning and development with other human resource development core processes such as Recruitment, Selection, and Placement (RSP), Performance Management (PM) and Rewards and Recognition (R&R), and initiatives of the department.

Accelerating towards the advancement of its delivery, the SDO Santiago City, fueled by its mantra, " Championing SCTEx: Sustaining the Culture of Teamwork towards Excellence," shall never cease in thriving and thinking forward towards the attainment of its goals.
